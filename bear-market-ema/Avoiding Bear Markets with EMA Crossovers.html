Abstract: Although sparsely observed, bear markets can put a serious dent in returns, even over a long-term investment horizon. Given economic uncertainty at the time of writing (December, 2018), there seems no better time than now to explore how one might make use of a simple technical indicator to protect their own portfolio from harm's way. I explore the use of the exponential moving average (EMA) as a useful indicator in identfying S&P 500 downturns. Additionally, using SPY as a basis, I quantify the efficacy of a simple market timing strategy (based on EMA crossover) versus buy and hold over an equivalent period from 1993-2018.

p < 0.10, so reject the null hypothesis that returns are the same between EMA 252 and EMA 90 crossover periods at the 90% confidence level.¶

Takeaway: The dotcom bubble and housing crisis could have been meandered with a simple analysis of EMA crossovers!¶

By avoiding these two downturns, our theoretical S&P 500 holdings gained an additional 313.45%, compared to a buy and hold strategy, over the period from 1993 - 2018.¶
